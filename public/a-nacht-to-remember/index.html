A Nacht to Remember

A one-shot holiday adventure for 5th-level characters (3 hours)

1. Introduction

1.1 Background

Deep in the frozen north lies the Cave of Punishment, an ancient place where Krampus—a demon who punishes the wicked during the darkest nights of winter—carries out his grim work. Above the caves stands the keep of Lord Klaus, a powerful archmage known for his arcane research and seasonal generosity. On this winter solstice night, something has gone terribly wrong.
Your head throbs as consciousness returns. The last thing you remember is clearing that disgusting well in Phandalin, the stench of the monsters you defeated still clinging to your clothes. But now... something is wrong. You find yourself sprawled on a frigid cave floor, dressed only in tattered children's nightclothes. Angry welts from birch switches crisscross your exposed skin. Your weapons, armor, and equipment are gone.

1.2 Adventure Hook

The adventure begins as the characters awaken in dire circumstances, with no memory of how they arrived...

2. The Caves of Punishment

2.1 Environment

Use these details progressively to build tension
Through the crystalline air, you hear the echoing sobs of children—some distant, some terrifyingly close. Their cries have an odd quality, as if the cave itself is weeping. Each welt from the birch switches pulses with supernatural chill, and your breath forms ice crystals that tinkle musically as they fall. In the distance, the sound of bells - not the cheerful kind - echoes through the caves.

Sensory Elements

  • Sight: Crystalline formations catch eerie light, shadows move wrong, occasional glimpse of small figures darting away
  • Sound:
    • Children's sobs that seem to come from multiple directions
    • Whispered pleas: "Please... I'll be good..."
    • Dripping icicles form rhythm with quiet weeping
    • Distant chains rattling in time with sniffles
    • Echoing whispers of "I'm sorry" that fade to silence
  • Feel: Bone-deep cold, static charge in air, occasional warm tear falling from above
  • Smell: Metallic frost, old blood, mineral dust, salt of tears

Atmosphere Shifts

  • Shadows lengthen unnaturally

  • Children's whispers echo distantly
  • Sobs grow louder near ritual markings
  • Crying intensifies as temperature drops

Dynamic Elements

  • Frost spreads visibly

2.2 Opening Scene

Initial Conditions

As your eyes adjust to the dim light, you find yourself in a large cavern roughly 60 feet across, illuminated by an eerie blue-green phosphorescence. The floor is slick with frost, and dark stains mark the center of the chamber. Thirteen stone columns rise from floor to ceiling, each carved with infernal runes. The air bites with an unnatural chill, and somewhere in the darkness, chains rattle ominously.
Starting State:
  • Characters begin with no equipment except torn children's sleepwear
  • Temperature is 20°F (-6��C); DC 10 Constitution saves every hour or gain exhaustion
  • Each character has 1d4 temporary hit points from numbness

Central Chamber

Notable Features:

  • Discarded birch switches, stained with blood
  • Frozen chains hanging from the columns
  • Four tunnel exits, one marked with child-sized footprints
Investigation Results:

Encounter - The Punished:

3.3 Ground Floor

The Foyer

The twisted remains of massive double doors hang from their hinges. Inside, carved stone dragons line the walls, and a large beholder's visage dominates the ceiling, its central eye following your movement. Three iron doors lead deeper into the keep - one to the west, one to the east, and one straight ahead. Seven unlit candelabras are spaced evenly around the room.
Dramatic Moment: When the party solves the candle puzzle, Klaus's magic activates - the beholder carving winks at them and holiday music begins playing from nowhere, but twisted and discordant.
Features:

  • Dragon Carvings: Conceal flame trap

3.4 Upper Floor

The Bedchambers

Rich crimson tapestries adorn the walls of this opulent chamber. A massive four-poster bed with red velvet curtains dominates the center, sized for someone of particularly jolly proportions. Through frosted windows, you see the lights of the town below and what appears to be a rooftop landing platform covered in strange marks. Before the bed stands an iron golem, its surface decorated with holiday motifs and what looks suspiciously like reindeer patterns.
Oh Shit Moment: Klaus's beard suddenly glows with radiant energy as Krampus approaches, and all the toys in the room animate to defend their creator. Grit starts stress-baking cookies that grant magical buffs to the party.
Features:

4.2 Battle Setup

Successful Tracking:
The snow-laden plaza lies before you, illuminated by flickering streetlamps. Through the falling snow, you see a towering figure with one human foot and one cloven hoof. Krampus stands near the center, chains rattling as he attempts to stuff another struggling victim into his magical sack. The demon hasn't noticed you yet.
Failed Tracking:
A bone-chilling laugh echoes across the snow-covered plaza as Krampus and his shadowy servants await your arrival. The demon's chains rattle ominously, and his magical sack writhes with the movement of those trapped within. "Naughty children," he growls, "coming to interrupt my work."
Dramatic Moment: The town's holiday decorations suddenly animate into a twisted parade, with Krampus conducting their movement using his chains like a maestro's baton.

    • Magical toys spring to life to defend him
    • Party finds their own childhood gifts in his records
